Building a professional brand is more than just listing projects; it's about demonstrating the same engineering rigor I apply to my clients' SaaS ecosystems. 

When I decided to rebuild [sakaru.com.np](https://sakaru.com.np), I chose a stack that prioritized precision, design excellence, and SEO performance. Here is how I built it.

### 1. Design with Stitch
I used **Stitch** to establish a high-premium visual identity. The goal was to create a design that felt both architecturally sound and visually inviting. Stitch allowed me to iterate quickly on the layout, ensuring that the "Kinetic Architect" theme was woven into every component through consistent tokens and sleek animations.

### 2. Implementation with Antigravity
The entire codebase was orchestrated using **Antigravity**, a powerful agentic coding assistant. Antigravity helped me bridge the gap between abstract design goals and concrete Astro components. From complex timeline animations using GSAP to the dynamic sitemap generation, Antigravity ensured the code was clean, performant, and maintainable.

### 3. SEO Audits with Claude
To ensure the site actually reaches global clients looking for "Zendesk Developers" and "Shopify Engineers," I leveraged **Claude** for rigorous SEO audits. Claude analyzed my CV and the site's content to identify critical gaps, helping me reconcile my professional timeline and optimize every meta tag for maximum search visibility.

### Why this matters
In the world of SaaS integration, the tools you choose define the quality of the output. By combining these advanced AI agents with my 10+ years of engineering experience, I was able to build a portfolio that isn't just a site—it's a demonstration of modern development efficiency.
